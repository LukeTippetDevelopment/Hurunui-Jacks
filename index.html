Whether you’re on a long New Zealand journey or a short weekend getaway, Hurunui Jacks is the perfect place to take a break along the West Coast Wilderness trail. Set on 15 acres of private, native forest land, with its own lake, and the Kaniere River on your doorstep, you’ll love the peace and tranquility that Hurunui Jacks has to offer.


The Tent

The Tent at Hurunui Jacks is an unforgettable glamping experience. Your luxury canvas tent rests on a private spot beside the Kaniere River, nestled amongst ancient virgin forest. The campsite consists of a large and roomy canvas tent, with a wood-burning stove. A fully covered and enclosed camp kitchen and bathroom is set just a few metres away. Enjoy a glass of wine and a soak in an outdoor bath under the stars. Then, tucked up in your comfortable bed, between linen sheets and under a goose down duvet, you'll be able to lie back and relax to the sound of the river. Lose yourself in the West Coast wilderness. Enjoy the peace and the ethereal feeling of being surrounded by ancient bush, in a truly unique landscape.

The Nest

The Nest at Hurunui Jacks is a cosy little haven. Situated close, but separate to the main house. Next to the Nest you will find your own private fire pit by the pond, the perfect setting to end your evening with. Marshmallows provided! The bath and shower are on the outside of the Nest, uncovered but private, on the deck. The water for the bath and shower is heated. It is important to make sure you are comfortable with this before you book. There is of course a separate toilet and hand basin inside. The Nest has a kitchen with hotplate and microwave so you can cook your own food. We have a BBQ grill available on request.

As an optional addition to your stay, we can provide everything you need to prepare your own light continental breakfast as well as plunger coffee freshly roasted on the property. We have a coffee roastery on the property, and roast a few times a week and you may be able to smell this from The Nest. You're more than welcome to come and watch Wendy roast. Once you leave Hokitika there's nowhere to buy groceries so we recommend you stock up when you're in town! The West Coast Wilderness Trail (part of the New Zealand wide cycle trail initiative) runs through our property. We have two bikes and helmets that you're welcome to use if you want to explore. We also have two kayaks and life jackets you can use to have a spin around our lake. Feel free to explore the property, surrounded by National Park and Dept. of Conservation land, take a walk through the native bush and down to the river, just breathe in the peace of it all. We have a few friendly resident birds who may come and visit you in the Nest, a duck called Daisy, a little Weka family, and some Pukeko. You're also likely to see Robins, Bell birds, Tom tits, tuis and swallows.
